Extraversion

By Author02 Aug 2019

Extraversion, referes to a state of being where someone rechanges or draws energy from being with another person, as opposed to being alone (introversion). As such, people who identify as extroverts tend to search for novel experiences and social connections that allows them to interact with other humans as much as possible. Someone who is highly an extrovert will likely feel bored, or even curious when they spend time alone.

Some of the sub-traits of extraversion are:
-Friendliness
-Energised
-Active
-Cheerful
-Excited

Research has shown that extroverts may be more successful compared to introverts, particularly in fields such as politics or entertainment, on the other hand, extroversion has also been linked to psychopathy and antisocial behaviour.
Recently, some researchers have proposed an "ambivert advantage" theorising that ambivert - individuals who fall in between of the 'introversion and extroversion" heirarchy scale - may be better off than tose at extreme.

True extroverts are often considered "the life of the party" but they can clash with more introverted types, who may find an extrovert's energy and enthuiasm overwhelming or difficult to tolerate.
