Neuroticism

By Author02 Aug 2019

Neuroticism is typically defined as a tendency towards anxiety, self-doubt, depression, shyness and other similar negative feelings towards yourself and others. Since all personality traits, including emotional instability exists on a spectrum, everyone is a little neurotic to some degree - some more than others.

Some specific traits for neuroticism can be:
-Concerned
-Uneasiness
-Conscious
-Vulnerable
-Saddened 
-Angry

For someone who is highly neurotic, its easy to feel trapped by the traits, maladaptive thought patterns, or struggle with depression or anxiety, both of which are more likely to occur in a highly neurotic individual. 
The evidence to make someone less neurotic is stirred, but recent studies suggests that personality traits are not set in stone, and can be changed over the course of a lifetime - particularly after a major life event like getting married or having a child. One can always choose to be engaged in a cognitive behavioural therapy or practice mindfulness to cope better with neuroticism.

In reality, neuroticism is rarely fun for anyone, though some research has shown that neuroticism can predict students' success, and maybe correlated with certain reproductive benefits. However, neuroticism may have provided evolutionary advantages, as well- paying more attention to negative outcomes, could have helped certain early humans survive over others. In present day, it maybe a better choicer to laugh at ones hang-ups, rather than run away from them.
